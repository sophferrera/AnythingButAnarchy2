What is the Federal Budget?

The United States federal budget comprises the spending and revenues of the U.S. federal government. The budget is the financial representation of the priorities of the government, reflecting historical debates and competing economic philosophies.


Under Trump's administration, a major portion of the federal budget is for defense. This number is more than the countries with the next ten highest defense budgets combined.

Trump's increase of more than $800 billion does not inclue the new stimulus package. The updated package is $436 billion in aid to states and cities to help alleviate budget shortfalls caused by Covid-19. The amount is a dramatic reduction from the over $900 billion that Democrats had included in the original Heroes Act.
